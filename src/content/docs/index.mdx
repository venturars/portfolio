    <h4>How I work</h4>
    <div>
      Over time, my approach to development has been shaped by working within
      real product environments, balancing technical ambition with practical
      constraints.
    </div>
    <div>
      Decisions are made in alignment with the realities of the product and the
      team, considering timelines, technical capacity, and both existing and
      potential technical debt.
    </div>
    <div>
      When changes introduce breaking implications, transitions are approached
      progressively rather than through abrupt replacement, maintaining legacy
      paths when needed to support continuity.
    </div>
    <div>
      Relevant decisions are documented, and technical documentation is updated
      when it contributes to long-term clarity. Architectural choices are shaped
      by the team's capabilities and delivery constraints, rather than by
      idealised models.
    </div>
    <div>
      For new features, architecture changes, or significant fixes, design notes
      are introduced when appropriate, without adding unnecessary process
      overhead.
    </div>
    <div>
      Implementation and architecture evolve in response to the product's
      circumstances, not in isolation from them.
    </div>
